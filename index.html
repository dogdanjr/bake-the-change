Why eight workstreams, not one list

Each workstream can move on its own — sponsorship outreach doesn't wait on volunteer recruitment, and social content doesn't wait on logistics. They only need to converge twice: Workstream 1's sponsor logos feed Workstream 5's signage, and Workstream 6's mission materials feed both Workstream 4's content and Workstream 5's table signage. Everything else runs in parallel.

The name itself is still open — Bake the Change is the working title, one of ten options Constance will choose from.

Still to confirm with Constance

  1. Event name — which of the ten options does she like (or a different one entirely)?
  2. Sponsorship tiers — what dollar levels/benefits should volunteers actually pitch? Nothing's fixed yet.
  3. Program location — the specific city/region in Africa the kits go to, so the pitch and signage say something real instead of just "Africa"
  4. Bakery commitments — of the 50+ target, how many are actually confirmed right now?
  5. Volunteers — how many does she think we'll actually need? Also whether she already has people lined up, or recruiting needs to happen too
  6. Venue/logistics — is Cal Ranch officially locked in, and are tables/canopies/chairs sourced or still needed?
  7. Printed materials — who's printing the letters/flyers volunteers will hand out, and by when?
  8. Name of charities — confirm the exact, correct names of Life Together Alliance and Village Threads (and any other beneficiaries) before they go on printed materials

WORKSTREAM 1 OF 8

Sponsorship & Fundraising Partners

Pre-event

Owner: Constance (relationships, pitch) + Daniel (routes, tracking, target list)

The core idea

  • This is won in person, not by letter or email — Constance's read is that a volunteer walking in and asking for the owner directly outperforms anything automated
  • Volunteers work assigned routes — a set of nearby businesses, a day, a time window
  • Every stop gets logged, whether it's a yes, a no, or "come back next week" — nobody re-visits the same business twice by accident
  • Anyone not reached (or non-committal) gets a follow-up visit or call one week later
  • A scraped/researched target list still matters — it's just feeding routes for volunteers to walk, not an automated letter blast

The system — who's going where

  • Rachel has Route A, Monday, 10am–1pm — three downtown stops
  • Michael has Route B, Tuesday, 1pm–4pm — the restaurant row
  • Routes are grouped by geography so a volunteer can walk or make one short drive, not criss-cross town
  • A shared tracker (template below) is the single source of truth — who's assigned where, who's been visited, what happened, when the follow-up is due

The pitch (30–45 seconds)

Walking in: "Hi, I'm volunteering with Bake the Change — a bake sale happening September 12th right here in Rexburg. Is the owner or manager around? I'd love to talk to them directly if I can."

If they're available: "Over 50 local bakeries are donating baked goods for one day to raise $65,000 for reusable hygiene kits for girls in Africa — right now a lot of girls miss school every month and eventually drop out for lack of basic supplies. One kit lasts 3–5 years and keeps a girl in class. We're asking local businesses to sponsor the event — your logo goes on our signage and social media, and it's tax-deductible. Even if sponsoring isn't the right fit today, we'd love to leave this for you to look over."

If they're not available: "No problem — can I leave this with you? It has everything they'd need, plus how to reach us. I'll check back in about a week."

Always: log the stop before moving on — even a "not interested" is useful, it keeps the next volunteer from knocking on the same door.

Ready-to-use templates

  • Volunteer Routes tracker — who, which route, which day, which businesses (seeded with an example)
  • Business follow-up log — seeded with real downtown Rexburg businesses as a starting list (addresses still need confirming)
  • Both open directly, or download and import into Google Sheets to share with volunteers live
